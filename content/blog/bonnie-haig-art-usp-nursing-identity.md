## The stated goal

Bonnie Haig was a painter with **18 paintings** showcased on her website and a stated goal of growing her email subscriber list.

## The actual problem

A full audit was conducted of the website, Instagram, and social presence from the perspective of both an ordinary visitor and a marketing analyst. The findings:

- Only **one reference to "special discounts"** anywhere on the site
- **No mention of selling** on Instagram
- **No Facebook business page** — eliminating the most powerful social advertising platform from the available options
- As an objective visitor: it was **not possible to determine whether the site sold anything at all**

## The reframe

Before answering the email list question, the task was reframed. The question was not *how to grow subscribers* — it was *what the underlying business goal was*.

- **Pure audience engagement** → one strategy
- **Painting sales** → a fundamentally different strategy

Without that clarity, driving traffic through any channel would not produce meaningful engagement or conversions.

## The USP recommendation

The central recommendation: build Bonnie's identity around her **nursing professional background**.

Paintings capturing a nurse's perspective — human vulnerability, caregiving, the emotional weight of healthcare — would connect to a specific, emotionally invested audience in a way that undifferentiated landscape or still life work cannot.

**Benchmark:** Leonid Afremov — cited as a standard for emotionally compelling visual art with a recognizable, specific identity.

An artist with a clear identity attracts an audience that self-selects. An artist without one has to fight for every visitor's attention every time.

## Supporting recommendations

- Establish a Facebook business page
- Make selling intent explicit on the homepage
- Build the email capture strategy around the positioned USP — not around a generic "join the list" prompt

## The outcome

Comprehensive marketing strategy assessment delivered: goal clarity, commercial intent signaling, social presence gaps, competitive differentiation, and a specific USP recommendation grounded in Bonnie's professional background. Implementation at the client's discretion.
