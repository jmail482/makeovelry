## The situation

Daniel operated **Cassie's (cassies.com.au)**, an adult entertainment business across five Australian locations: New South Wales (Sydney), Victoria, and three additional states. He was ranking adequately for primary metropolitan targets but sought stronger visibility in smaller city and suburban markets.

**Engagement: September 26, 2022.**

## The constraints

The adult industry classification created documented limitations:

- **Citation directories:** many declined to work with adult content businesses
- **Backlink vendors:** same restriction applied
- **GMB verification:** required exterior business signage photography — difficult for a business without a conventional retail storefront[^1]
- **Competitors:** were including search keywords directly in their GMB business name field, in violation of Google's guidelines[^2] — and Google was not acting on reports despite them being filed

## What the initial audit found

The GMB profile was not optimised — the description was too short and lacked keyword coverage. Facebook and LinkedIn business pages were both absent, removing two significant authority backlink signals that don't carry adult content restrictions. BrightLocal citation management was already underway for Sydney.[^3]

## What Jonathan recommended

- **GMB description:** Expand and optimise within category limitations
- **Facebook and LinkedIn business pages:** Establish both — these platforms accept adult entertainment business pages and provide clean, high-authority backlink signals
- **BrightLocal:** Continue citation completion for Sydney
- **Competitor name-stuffing:** Jonathan addressed this directly. Google was not enforcing its own guidelines. His advice: **do not replicate the practice** — the long-term enforcement risk outweighs the short-term ranking advantage[^2]
- **Secondary markets without a physical GBP listing:** On-page location targeting and blog posts optimised for location-plus-service keyword combinations

## What this demonstrates

A standard local SEO template — citation blast, GMB optimisation, backlink outreach — doesn't apply to a business category where most of those channels are closed. The correct approach is constraint mapping first: what's available, what isn't, and what can realistically be done within those limits. Honest assessment of what Google wasn't enforcing — rather than recommending a tactic based on observed competitor behaviour — was part of that.
