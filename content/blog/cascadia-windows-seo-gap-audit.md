## The company

**Cascadia Windows & Doors** — Langley, BC manufacturer of high-performance fiberglass windows, window wall systems, storefront glazing, patio doors, and the Cascadia Clip® thermally broken cladding attachment system. Founded 2008 by building envelope engineers who had managed the Pacific Northwest's leaky condo crisis [cascadiawindows.com/company/about][^1]. 150 employees. 90,000 sq ft facility. Projects in every Canadian province and territory and nearly every U.S. state [cascadiawindows.com/company/blog/cascadia-growth-investment][^2].

Product credentials: Passive House Institute (PHI) certified. Passive House Institute U.S. (PHIUS) certified. Architizer A+ Product Award, 2024 — Universal Series Window Wall [LeadIQ][^3]. World's first fiberglass window wall, 2019 [businesswire.com][^4]. World's first fiberglass storefront glazing system, 2020 [businesswire.com][^5]. World's first verified Environmental Product Declaration (EPD) for windows, doors, and window wall, 2024 [cascadiawindows.com][^1].

Growth investment: MKB Equity Partners (lead), Canada Growth Fund, Blue Earth Capital — announced 2026 [cascadiawindows.com/company/blog/cascadia-growth-investment][^2].

*SERP and keyword data collected June 2, 2026. Tools: Serper [^6], Mangools [^7], SearchVolume.io [^8].*

## The headline finding

Cascadia ranks on page one for **two of seven priority commercial queries** [Serper API, Jun 2026][^6]. The five they don't appear on are the queries their architect, developer, and general contractor buyers run before a product ever gets specified.

Their CMS is Smallbox 8.0.0 — a proprietary Canadian platform whose meta generator tag is exposed sitewide (`meta-generator: Smallbox CMS 8.0.0 www.smallbox.ca`) [site crawl, Jun 2026][^9]. No public plugin ecosystem. No third-party SEO tool integrations. WordPress with Rank Math or Yoast gives a non-developer full control over every on-page SEO field. Smallbox gives whatever the agency that built the site configured.

Enough branded search volume exists that Google has normalized the pairing. The brand recognition is real. Unbranded discovery — architects who don't yet know Cascadia exists — isn't being captured.

## The competitive gap

The "passive house windows canada" result is the sharpest example. Innotech Windows & Doors ranks first [Serper, Jun 2026][^6]. EkoBuilt, Euroline Windows, Vetta Windows, and INTUS Windows complete page one [Serper, Jun 2026][^6]. Cascadia holds PHI and PHIUS certified product configurations, Canadian manufacturing, and province-spanning project history. They are not on the page.

On "storefront glazing vs curtain wall" — a pure architect research query, the query someone runs when deciding which system to specify — Building Enclosure Online, a UK consulting firm, an Autodesk forum thread, and an ARE exam study community all rank [Serper, Jun 2026][^6]. Cascadia launched the world's first fiberglass storefront glazing system in 2020 [businesswire.com][^5]. Their presence in that conversation: zero.

Fibertec, a direct fiberglass window competitor, holds position one for "commercial fiberglass windows manufacturer" [Serper, Jun 2026][^6]. Milgard, Marvin, and Pella — residential-skewed brands with less technical differentiation for commercial Passive House applications — also appear above Cascadia on that page.

## The buyer search behaviour

Cascadia's B2B buyers search in two modes. The first is **spec-driven**: they know the standard they need to hit (Passive House, LEED, energy code) and are looking for qualifying products. Serper autocomplete data across 25 B2B seed terms surfaced "passive house certified windows canada," "LEED certified windows," "thermally broken commercial windows," and "energy efficient windows for commercial buildings" as active query clusters [Serper autocomplete, Jun 2026][^6].

The second mode is **comparison-stage**: earlier in the specification process, they research systems to understand what exists. "Storefront glazing vs curtain wall," "difference between curtain wall and window wall," "fiberglass vs aluminum windows commercial," and "what is high performance glazing" all surface in autocomplete [Serper autocomplete, Jun 2026][^6]. These are questions Cascadia's technical team answers every week in CEU webinars. None of that content is indexed.

## The root cause

Content volume is not the problem. The CEU webinar library — accredited by AIA, AIBC, OAA, AAA, BOABC, ASTTBC, and BC Housing CPD [cascadiawindows.com/resources/webinars][^1] — covers exactly the topics their buyers search. Webinar titles include "Active Approach to Passive House Windows," "Beyond the Frame: High-Performance Fenestration," and "Window of Opportunity: Leveraging Passive House Windows in Commercial & Residential Buildings" [cascadiawindows.com/resources/webinars][^1]. PDF slide decks. Invisible to Google.

The R-Value/U-Value calculator, NAFS calculator, and Cascadia Clip spacing calculator sit on utility pages with no SEO scaffolding [cascadiawindows.com/tools][^1]. Each one is a natural anchor for content that would rank for technical specification queries.

## Three closeable gaps

**Passive House pillar page.** A dedicated page built around PHI/PHIUS certification, Canadian manufacturing, and province-spanning Passive House project history would contest the "passive house windows canada" cluster directly. The competitors holding those positions are not more qualified to own them.

**Storefront vs. curtain wall vs. window wall comparison guide.** A technically rigorous comparison — written from a building science perspective — would rank for the architect research queries that currently route to trade publications, UK consultancies, and Autodesk forums. Cascadia invented the fiberglass storefront glazing category. They are not the authoritative online voice on how it compares to the alternatives.

**CEU-to-content conversion.** Existing webinar material, systematically turned into structured indexed pages, would populate the comparison-stage keyword cluster without requiring new research. The subject matter expertise is already documented. It needs to be published in a format Google can crawl.

## What this demonstrates

Cascadia's search presence reflects brand-only SEO: strong on the product terms they invented, absent on the category terms their buyers use before they know which brand to specify. For a company expanding North American reach on institutional capital, unbranded search is the highest-intent, lowest-cost acquisition channel they are currently not operating.

The five missing positions are not a keyword difficulty problem. All five queries are low-to-moderate difficulty with no entrenched domain holding multiple top-three positions. They are a content publication problem — and the content to solve it already exists inside the organization.
