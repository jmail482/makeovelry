## The opportunity

The search query *where's my cell phone* generated substantial monthly search volume — phone misplacement is a near-universal, recurring experience. Jonathan's competitive research in 2013 found only two websites providing substantive solutions. The remainder of the SERP was thin, low-quality, or irrelevant. No established brand dominated the space.

The query carried clear AdSense monetization potential: sustained high volume at near-zero customer acquisition cost, with an audience whose immediate problem made them receptive to adjacent suggestions.

## The build

Jonathan built the site on WordPress under the author username Jon4822 and wrote all content himself. The content architecture addressed the core user problem across four distinct H2 sections, each covering a separate phone-location method: calling the device from another phone, locating it via internet-based call services, and two additional approaches targeting different intent layers within the same query cluster.

Semantic optimization tools were applied to calibrate keyword relevance across the content. A PR5 anchor text backlink was acquired to reinforce the ranking position.

## The result

The site ranked in the **top 3 organic positions**, achieving the **number-two position at peak** — outranking the two authority-level competitor websites that had previously owned the space.

Google AdSense generated **weekly income for approximately 15 months** from sustained organic traffic.

## What ended it

Google launched its native **Find My Phone** feature directly within search results. The query intent was absorbed at the SERP level. Third-party sites providing the same answer lost their traffic source. The site remained live from August 2013 through September 2017 (27 Wayback Machine captures documented).

## The lesson

Ranking an exact-match domain against two weak incumbents on a high-volume informational query is repeatable. The risk is platform dependency: when Google decides a query intent is better served by a SERP feature, third-party content loses. The 15-month window was real income — but the exit wasn't voluntary.
