## The cause

**Bill 226** — Canadian federal legislation supporting Multiple Myeloma research funding via the proposed Canadian CMB Foundation. Rob Tetrault — wealth advisor, philanthropist, the engagement's anchor — built a campaign calling Canadian MPs to support the bill. The SEO outcome was the byproduct of a real cause-driven PR effort, not a vanity SEO play.

## The pipeline

1. **The wire:** EIN Presswire — a paid press-release distribution service ($15/release)[^1] — picks up the campaign story and syndicates it across regional news, financial publications, and policy-tracking aggregators.
2. **The volume effect:** EIN's syndication produces dozens of editorial pickups within hours. Google sees a coordinated topical signal: "this query has authoritative recent coverage."
3. **The structured content:** The release itself was written with FAQ-style structure — "What is Bill 226?" / "Who supports it?" / "How can citizens act?" — which Google's snippet extractor reliably parses.[^2]
4. **The owned property:** Rob's site (robtetrault.com at the time) carried matching long-form coverage with the same Q&A pairs, which Google ultimately surfaced as the Featured Snippet.

## The result

**Position 0 (Featured Snippet)** on the primary query around the issue. Held for approximately **14 months** — from spring 2024 through mid-2025. The snippet didn't just rank — it owned the answer that Google read aloud to voice-search users asking about the bill.

## What it actually cost

- One EIN Presswire wire: $15[^1]
- Long-form coverage on robtetrault.com: existing site, no incremental cost
- FAQ structure: 1–2 hours of editorial work
- The cause itself: Rob's own

**Total marginal SEO spend: ~$15.**

## Why most consultants don't run this play

It requires a real cause. The pipeline only works when the underlying story is true, the wire carries an actual newsworthy claim, and the owned property has genuine long-form coverage to anchor the snippet to. The shortcut version — buy a wire, syndicate generic content — doesn't work. Google's snippet extractor distinguishes genuine policy/news coverage from astroturfed PR.[^2]

## What ended it

The Featured Snippet stopped surfacing in mid-2025 — not because of an algorithm change, but because Rob's domain was migrated/rebranded after the wealth advisory engagement wound down. Google processed the migration over several weeks, the canonical URL changed, and the snippet capture didn't persist.[^3]
