## The business

Peak 15 Systems builds booking, itinerary, and back-office management software for multi-day tour operators and destination management companies (DMCs). Their product is purpose-built for a specific operator type — multi-day, high-complexity, high-customization — that generic travel software platforms underserve.

*SpyFu API audit, March 2026.*

## The baseline

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Monthly organic visits | ~50[^1] |
| Near-page-1 keywords (ranks 11–20) | 8[^1] |
| "Tour operator software" rank | #14 (page 2)[^1] |
| Commercial landing pages | 0 |
| Competitor keyword overlap (top competitor) | 68 shared keywords[^1] |
| Peak 15 ranking footprint | Brand + 1 blog post |

One blog post — **"What Software Do Travel Agents Use"** — drove virtually all organic traffic at approximately 25 clicks/month, ranking #8 for "travel agency back office software" (220 searches/month).[^1] The homepage ranked #1 for the brand name. Everything else was functionally invisible.

## The competitive gap

SpyFu API confirmed 10 competing domains with shared keyword overlap. Top competitors by shared keywords and estimated monthly traffic:[^1]

- trawex.com: 68 shared keywords, 8,000+ monthly visits
- travefy.com: 65 shared keywords, 12,000+ monthly visits
- travelopro.com: 66 shared keywords, 6,000+ monthly visits
- wptravelengine.com: 53 shared keywords, 15,000+ monthly visits

Peak 15: brand + 1 blog post.

**None of these competitors have a better product for multi-day operators than Peak 15.** Travefy's single itinerary template page generates more monthly clicks than Peak 15's entire website combined.[^1] The gap is structural, not competitive.

## Five reasons the site is invisible to buyers

**Gap 1 — No commercial landing pages.** Zero dedicated pages for buyer-intent keywords. No /tour-operator-software, no /itinerary-builder, no /travel-crm.

**Gap 2 — 10× content volume shortfall.** 5–10 blog posts against 50–200 at leading competitors.[^1] The one existing article proves the channel works.

**Gap 3 — Homepage earns zero non-brand traffic.** Ranks #1 for "peak 15." Ranks #14 for "tour operator software."

**Gap 4 — No comparison pages.** In B2B SaaS, product-intent landing pages and comparison pages together generate approximately 70% of organic leads.[^2] Peak 15 had zero presence in either category.

**Gap 5 — Conversion friction throughout.** No demo video, no ROI calculator, no pricing signals, no client logos. 73% of B2B buyers watch video before purchasing.[^3]

## Keyword architecture

**Near-page-1 (addressable in 60–90 days, SpyFu API March 2026):[^1]**
- travel agency back office software — 220 searches/month, rank #8
- tour operator software — 44+ searches/month, rank #14
- travel agent tools — 110 searches/month, rank #15
- group travel management software — 80 searches/month, rank #15

**Not ranking at all (SpyFu API March 2026):[^1]**
- small tour operators software — 440 searches/month, difficulty 12
- travel agent booking system — 250 searches/month, difficulty: easy
- tour operator booking software — 125 searches/month, difficulty 19
- tour operator back office software — 70 searches/month, difficulty 7

## The seven revenue pages

| Page | Traffic Potential | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| /tour-operator-software | 200–700/month | 19 |
| /tour-operator-booking-software | 100–300/month | 19 |
| /tour-operator-back-office-software | 80–200/month | 7 — fastest win |
| /itinerary-builder-software | 150–400/month | Medium |
| /travel-proposal-software | 80–200/month | Low |
| /best-tour-operator-software | 200–500/month | Medium |
| /peak15-vs-tourwriter + /peak15-vs-travefy | 100–300/month combined | Low |

At a 3% demo conversion rate — standard for B2B SaaS[^4] — these seven pages represent 27–78 additional demo requests per month from pages that did not yet exist.

## The projection

| Timeline | Monthly Organic Traffic | Demo Requests |
|---|---|---|
| Current | ~50[^1] | <2 |
| 3 months | ~600 | ~15 |
| 6 months | ~2,500 | ~62 |
| 12 months | ~10,000 | ~250 |

Phase 1 alone — expanding one blog post, adding schema, and building two landing pages — was projected to more than double organic traffic within 60–90 days. The niche is structurally undercompeted. The product is already strong. The entire gap is architectural.
