## The window

In 2008, the electronic cigarette category was in its early commercial phase. Consumer awareness was growing, affiliate programs were emerging, but the review and comparison content landscape was almost entirely empty. Few credible, well-structured affiliate review sites existed for the category.

Jonathan identified this as an early-adopter window: genuine and growing consumer demand, available affiliate commission structures, and minimal content competition at the point of entry.

## The build

Jonathan built the site on Weebly and structured it as a multi-section affiliate review property. Navigation covered:

- HOME
- E-CIG BLOG
- ABOUT ELECTRONIC CIGARETTES
- BUYING ELECTRONIC CIGARETTE BRANDS
- ELECTRONIC CIGARETTE COUPONS
- Dedicated Greensmoke page

Ten brands were reviewed and written up by Jonathan: **Greensmoke** (featured and recommended), Vaporzone, Whitecloud, Premium E Cigarette, No Safe Cig, Volcano, eSmoke, VyCig, South Beach Smoke, and Blurip.

Content covered product comparison methodology, buyer guidance, brand ranking rationale, and long-form educational content on e-cigarettes as a category. Jonathan executed all on-site SEO, submitted the site to online directories and search engines, and integrated third-party display advertising alongside affiliate links as a secondary passive revenue stream.

## The result

The site established an audience organically within the niche. **Several dozen readers purchased electronic cigarette products through the affiliate links**, with a portion becoming repeat buyers. Affiliate commission income was generated consistently for **approximately 24 months**. Third-party display advertising (including a Gold IRA Future banner visible in the Weebly archive) provided a secondary passive income layer independent of affiliate conversions.

## Why it worked

Entry timing was the primary driver. The same site built two years later — after established review properties, affiliate aggregators, and brand-owned content had moved into the space — would not have achieved the same organic position or income duration. The early-adopter window closed as the category saturated.
